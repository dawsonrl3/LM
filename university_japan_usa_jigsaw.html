Full Article

Paragraph 1
University life is very different in Japan and the United States. One student studied in both countries and shared his story. He found five main differences in the way students live and study. These differences include clothes, clubs, lessons, housing, and how hard they must study.

Paragraph 2
The first difference is fashion. In Japanese schools, students must wear uniforms for many years. When they go to university, they like to buy nice clothes and look good. In the United States, students usually wear simple and comfortable clothes. They often wear sweatshirts or T-shirts from the university shop.

Paragraph 3
Next, extracurricular clubs are different. Japanese universities have groups called "circles" where students go to have fun and meet friends. These are very social groups. In American universities, sports teams and clubs are more serious. Students must practice very hard every day, or they spend most of their time studying.

Paragraph 4
Academic pressure also changes between the two countries. In Japan, high school students study very hard for entrance exams. Once they enter university, they often have more free time. In the United States, university is very difficult every day. Students have to write many reports and read many books every week.

Paragraph 5
Living arrangements are another big difference. Many Japanese students live alone in their own apartments. They enjoy having their own space for the first time. In the United States, most students live in dorms. They usually share a room with another student. This is a good way to make friends, but it can be difficult if they are very different.

Paragraph 6
Finally, classroom participation is different. In Japan, the teacher speaks and the students listen quietly. Students do not usually ask questions during the lesson. In the United States, students talk a lot. They ask questions and have debates with the teacher and other students. Both systems help students learn in different ways.
