Full Article

Introduction
Many experts are worried because young people's test scores are falling for the first time in many years. Students today are doing worse on academic tests than their parents did. For example, Dr. Jared Cooney Horvath says Gen Z is the first generation in modern history to get lower scores on standardized tests than the generation before them. This decline is happening in Japan too. A recent survey showed that English scores for junior high students dropped by 22.9 points compared to a few years ago.

Paragraph 2
Using digital devices too much is making it harder for students to learn deeply. Many young people spend too much time on their phones instead of studying. Dr. Horvath says that teenagers spend more than half of the time they are awake staring at a screen. Because of this, students are reading fewer books, which makes it hard for them to understand difficult information. In the United States, the number of older students who read every day has fallen by more than 40% over the last twenty years.

Paragraph 3
New digital habits are changing how young people think and focus. Scrolling through social media for a long time makes it difficult for students to focus on long stories or schoolwork. Doomscrolling can cause high anxiety and stop students from thinking carefully. "Doomscrolling" is a colloquialism that means spending a long time looking at bad or upsetting news on a phone. Also, using too much technology in the classroom may actually hurt learning. Research across 80 countries shows that when schools use a lot of digital technology, student performance often goes down.
