Full Article

Paragraph 1
Many young people in Generation Z (born between 1990 and 2010) have mental health problems today. A new survey of more than 1,000 people aged 18 to 24 shows that 42% of Gen Z have a mental health diagnosis. This means almost half of the people in this group have a formal medical record of a condition like anxiety or depression. Life has been difficult for them because of many big changes and "upheaval" in the world during their short lives.

Paragraph 2
Young people use different ways to manage these challenges and feel better. According to the survey, the most common problem is anxiety, followed by depression, ADHD, and PTSD. To help with these problems, 20% of Gen Z go to therapy to talk to a professional. Also, about 60% of these young people take medication to help them manage their mental health every day.

Paragraph 3
There are many reasons why young people feel stressed and worried about their lives. The COVID-19 pandemic was very hard for 70% of Gen Z because it made them feel lonely and sad. Today, 85% of young people worry about the future. Their biggest worries are about money and the economy, but they also feel stress about politics, the environment, and finding work. Because of this, 90% of them do not feel ready for success.

Paragraph 4
Finally, Gen Z believes they are very good at talking about their feelings and problems. About 79% of them think their generation is the best at addressing mental health. They feel comfortable talking to friends, brothers, sisters, or parents. However, they do not like talking about their struggles with a boss or a stranger. They also think older people, like "Baby Boomers," are the worst at talking about mental health.
